Phishing Attack Types & Prevention

Learn about different phishing techniques and how to protect yourself

Email Phishing

Mass emails sent to thousands of users, appearing to come from legitimate companies.

Red Flags:
  • Generic greetings ("Dear Customer")
  • Urgent action required
  • Poor grammar and spelling
  • Suspicious sender addresses
Prevention:
  • Verify sender email addresses
  • Don't click suspicious links
  • Use spam filters
  • Report suspicious emails
Spear Phishing

Targeted attacks on specific individuals or organizations using personalized information.

Red Flags:
  • Personalized with your name
  • References to your work/company
  • Appears to come from colleagues
  • Requests sensitive information
Prevention:
  • Verify requests through other channels
  • Be suspicious of urgent requests
  • Check email headers
  • Use multi-factor authentication
Whaling

Targets high-profile executives and senior management with sophisticated attacks.

Red Flags:
  • Appears to come from CEO/executive
  • Requests large financial transfers
  • Mentions confidential projects
  • Urgent wire transfer requests
Prevention:
  • Verify with executive directly
  • Follow company procedures
  • Require multiple approvals
  • Train staff on whaling tactics
SMS Phishing (Smishing)

Phishing attacks sent via text messages, often claiming to be from banks or delivery services.

Red Flags:
  • Unexpected delivery notifications
  • Bank account alerts
  • Clickable links in SMS
  • Requests for personal info
Prevention:
  • Don't click SMS links
  • Contact companies directly
  • Use official apps
  • Report suspicious texts
Voice Phishing (Vishing)

Phone-based attacks where scammers impersonate legitimate organizations.

Red Flags:
  • Automated voice messages
  • Requests for account details
  • Threats of account closure
  • Caller ID spoofing
Prevention:
  • Hang up immediately
  • Call companies back directly
  • Never share personal info
  • Use call blocking apps

General Prevention Strategies

Security Best Practices
  • Use strong, unique passwords
  • Enable multi-factor authentication
  • Keep software updated
  • Use reputable antivirus software
  • Regularly backup important data
  • Be cautious with public Wi-Fi
Personal Vigilance
  • Think before clicking
  • Verify sender identities
  • Check for HTTPS in URLs
  • Be suspicious of urgent requests
  • Don't share personal information
  • Trust your instincts
